The Gratitude Exercise

Our study showed that gratitude boosts happiness for a variety of reasons - which is why Prof. Sonja Lyubomirsky calls it a "meta strategy". This exercise involves showing gratitude to a person who has had a positive influence in your life.

The first step involves thinking of someone who had a positive influence on your life. This person could be someone from your past (e.g., teacher, mentor) or present (e.g., friend, spouse). It is ideal to think of someone who is still alive. If you prefer to write your letter to someone who has passed on, read the letter to someone who knew this person.
